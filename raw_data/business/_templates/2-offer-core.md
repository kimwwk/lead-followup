# Offer Core
**Marketing Agent Kit · Brand Brain — File 2 of 3**

Defines **what you sell, who it's for, what it's proven, and how you talk about it.** The agent reads this to generate conversion-aligned content.

> 📌 **How to fill this out** — Be specific everywhere. Vague answers produce vague content. If you have numbers, use them. If you have exact client quotes, use them. This is the file the agent reads when it needs to mention your offer, handle objections, or write a CTA.

## 1 · Your Offer

### Offer name
- _e.g. APS Performance Coaching_

### One-sentence promise
> 📌 Complete this sentence: **"I help [specific person] achieve [specific result] through [unique mechanism]."** This is the line the agent uses in offer mentions, transitions, and CTAs.

> **Example — fitness coach**
> "I help time-poor professionals over 35 build a body they're proud of — without overhauling their life — through a programme that adapts to their actual week, not their ideal one."

### The mechanism — what makes it work
> 📌 The unique approach, system, or method that makes your offer different. NOT your result — *how* you deliver it. Named mechanisms are more memorable and harder to copy.

> **Example — fitness coach**
> "Week 0 Schedule Audit: before a single session is written, we map the client's travel patterns, energy windows, meeting load, and stress cycles. Every programme starts from their real week. Three variants are built in — Full Week, Minimum Effective, Travel Week — and the client picks Sunday night based on what's coming."

## 4 · CTA Language

> 📌 How you actually ask for the action. The agent pulls from here for every CTA. It should feel natural and on-brand — not pushy, not passive.

### Primary CTA
> 📌 The main action you want someone to take — one action only (DM a keyword, click a link, book a call).
- **Primary action** — _e.g. DM me the word SCHEDULE_
- **Where it goes** — _e.g. a free 15-minute audit call booking page_

### CTA style — soft or direct?
> 📌 Soft CTAs invite; direct CTAs instruct. Neither is wrong — it depends on your brand. Pick one and explain.

- [ ] **Soft** — "If this resonates, DM me…" / "If you want to learn more…"
- [ ] **Direct** — "DM me SCHEDULE right now." / "Comment AUDIT and I'll send it."
