# Positioning Core
**Marketing Agent Kit · Brand Brain — File 1 of 3**

Defines **who you help, why you exist, and how you talk about it.** The Marketing Agent reads this before generating any content.

> 📌 **How to fill this out** — Fill every section as completely as possible. The more specific and honest your answers, the more the agent sounds like *you*, not a generic AI. Use the examples as a guide but write in your own voice. Don't write what sounds professional — write what's true.

## 1 · Who You Help — ICP Profile

> 📌 ICP = Ideal Client Profile: the specific person your content is written for. The more precisely you describe them, the more your audience feels like you read their mind.

### Demographics & identity
- **Age range** — _e.g. 32–45_
- **Occupation / title** — _e.g. senior marketing manager at a B2B SaaS company_
- **Life stage** — _e.g. recently promoted, first child, relocated to a new city_
- **Identity label** (what they call themselves) — _e.g. "I'm a founder", "I'm a working mum", "I'm a high achiever"_

### Their primary pain point
> 📌 The most important field in this document. Write the pain in the **exact words** your client would use — not the clinical version. The agent lifts phrases directly from here.

> **Example — fitness coach**
> "I've started and stopped so many times I've honestly started to believe the problem is me. Every programme works for two weeks and then life gets in the way and I'm back at square one."

## 2 · Your Origin Story

> 📌 Your origin story is the most powerful positioning tool you have — it explains why you built what you built, and why you're the right person to help. Used for authority content and proof beats.

### The before state
> 📌 Where were you before you figured this out? Be specific — numbers, timeframes, emotional details.

> **Example — fitness coach**
> "Six years into my coaching career, 40% of my clients would disappear after month two. I thought I was the problem — not skilled enough, not motivating enough."

> _…_

### The turning point
> 📌 What happened that changed everything? A moment, a realisation, a client conversation, a failure that led to a breakthrough.

> _…_

### The after state
> 📌 What changed as a result? Include specific metrics or transformations if you have them.

> **Example — fitness coach**
> "Retention went from 40% to 87%. Same clients. Same intensity. I just stopped writing programmes for their best week and started writing for their worst."

> _…_

### The lesson (what you learned that most people don't know)
> 📌 The insight your content comes back to again and again — the core of your positioning. One or two sentences max.

> **Example — fitness coach**
> "Consistency doesn't come from discipline. It comes from a programme that survives contact with your actual life."

## 4 · Your Language

> 📌 Language is voice. This section keeps every output sounding like you — and avoids words that break the spell.

### Words & phrases your audience uses
> 📌 How your clients describe their problem, goal, and frustration — the actual phrases you hear on calls, in DMs, in comments. Used verbatim.

> **Example — fitness coach**
> "falling off", "back at square one", "I just need to be more consistent", "life gets in the way", "I know what to do, I just can't make myself do it"

> _…_

### Words & phrases to NEVER use
> 📌 Language that feels off-brand, corporate, or generic for you — what makes content sound like everyone else.

> **Example — fitness coach**
> "smash your goals", "accountability partner", "lifestyle transformation", "it's a marathon not a sprint", "the journey"

> _…_

### Your branded frameworks & named concepts
> 📌 Named methodologies, frameworks, systems, phases. Names make content uniquely yours and shareable. No names yet? Give them names here.

> **Example — fitness coach**
> "Schedule Stress Test" — testing a programme against the client's worst week. / "Minimum Effective Routine" — the baseline that survives any week. / "Travel Week Variant" — the adapted version for travel.

> _…_

### Power phrases unique to your brand
> 📌 Phrases that ARE your voice — lines you say often, lines that land. Your verbal fingerprints.

> **Example — fitness coach**
> "still training 12 months later" / "built for your actual week" / "survives contact with your real life"

> _…_
